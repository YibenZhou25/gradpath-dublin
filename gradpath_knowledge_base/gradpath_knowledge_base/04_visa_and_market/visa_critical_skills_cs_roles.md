# Critical Skills Occupations List · CS-Relevant Roles (2026)

> Curated subset of the Critical Skills Occupations List (CSOL) focused on roles
> relevant to graduate-level CS Master's students in Ireland.
>
> **Last verified: April 2026.** Reflects the post-1-March-2026 employment
> permit threshold update under the Department of Enterprise, Tourism and
> Employment (DETE) Roadmap.
>
> **This is a working summary, not authoritative.** Always verify current CSOL
> contents at https://enterprise.gov.ie/

## What the Critical Skills List is

The Critical Skills Occupations List (CSOL) identifies job roles for which
Ireland has identified a strategic skills shortage. Roles on the CSOL benefit
from:

1. **Eligibility for the Critical Skills Employment Permit (CSEP)** — the most
   favourable permit type for non-EU workers
2. **Waiver of the Labour Market Needs Test** that General Employment Permits
   require (saving 28+ days of advertising plus administrative work)
3. **No 50/50 EEA-workforce rule** applies to CSEP applications
4. **Faster path to Stamp 4** — eligible after 21 months on CSEP, vs five years
   on the General Employment Permit
5. **Immediate family reunification** — spouses and dependants can join from day
   one, and spouses receive Stamp 1G permitting work without their own
   employment permit

For an international CS graduate, having a target role on the CSOL is the single
most significant factor making employer sponsorship straightforward.

## CS-relevant CSOL occupations (2026)

The categories below are the ones most likely to be relevant to a CS Master's
graduate. **Verify each against the current CSOL** — list contents and
classification codes can change.

### Software development and engineering

Roles consistently on the CSOL:
- Software Developer / Software Engineer
- Senior Software Engineer
- Full Stack Developer
- Backend Developer
- Frontend Developer
- Mobile Application Developer
- Embedded Software Engineer
- Web and Multimedia Developer
- Database Designer / Administrator
- Systems Analyst
- Programmer (general)

What graduates should know:
- **Software engineering has been on the CSOL since 2015 and remains there in
  2026.** This is the most stable category.
- Almost all standard SWE roles at multinational employers fall into this
  category
- The CSOL classification is by occupation (using SOC 2010 codes), not by
  employer
- Job titles vary by employer; the underlying occupation is what matters

### Data and machine learning

Roles consistently on the CSOL:
- Data Engineer
- Data Scientist
- Data Analyst (subject to specific specialisation criteria)
- Machine Learning Engineer
- AI Engineer
- Big Data specialists

What graduates should know:
- ML/AI roles have been highly sought after on the CSOL throughout the 2020s
- Pure dashboarding/reporting roles without significant engineering content
  may sometimes fall outside the strict CSOL definition — read the JD carefully
  for the engineering depth

### Cloud, platform, and DevOps

Roles consistently on the CSOL:
- Cloud Engineer
- DevOps Engineer
- Site Reliability Engineer (SRE)
- Platform Engineer
- Cloud Architect (typically more senior)

What graduates should know:
- These roles often require demonstrable hands-on experience with major cloud
  platforms (AWS, Azure, GCP)
- Internships and substantial coursework projects with cloud deployment
  artefacts strengthen the case at graduate level

### Cybersecurity and ICT security

Roles consistently on the CSOL:
- ICT Security Specialist
- Cybersecurity Analyst
- Information Security Engineer
- Security Architect (more senior)
- Penetration Tester

What graduates should know:
- Cybersecurity has consistently been on the CSOL due to recognised skills
  shortage
- Specialised certifications (CISSP, CompTIA Security+, OSCP) may be expected
  even at graduate level

### ICT management and adjacent professional roles

Often on the CSOL:
- ICT Project Manager
- ICT Managers (general)
- IT Business Analyst (case-by-case)

For a CS graduate moving into a product-management track, CSOL eligibility
will depend on the specific job classification used by the employer's HR.

## Salary thresholds for CSEP (effective 1 March 2026)

Two pathway sub-routes within CSEP:

**Standard CSEP (with relevant degree):**
- **€40,904** minimum annual base salary
- Required if the role is on the CSOL and the candidate has a relevant degree

**Recent graduate CSEP (graduated within previous 12 months, from any recognised institution with a relevant degree):**
- **€36,848** minimum annual base salary
- Re-introduced from 1 March 2026 to support graduate hiring

**CSEP via high-salary route (without degree, with relevant experience):**
- **€68,911** minimum annual base salary
- Required if the candidate lacks a relevant degree but has substantial experience
- Used less commonly for graduate-level applications

### Key implication for graduates

For a CS Master's graduate from a Dublin university transitioning from Stamp 1G
to CSEP within 12 months of graduation, **the relevant threshold is €36,848,
not €40,904**. Most multinational tech employers' graduate offers exceed both
thresholds comfortably, but for offers from smaller Irish employers, this
distinction can be the difference between qualifying and not.

## Typical roles NOT on the CSOL

Roles that CS graduates sometimes consider but which are typically NOT on the
Critical Skills List include:

- Quality Assurance / Manual Testing roles (depending on classification — some
  technical SDET roles do qualify)
- IT Support / Helpdesk roles
- General consulting roles without a specific technical specialisation
- Sales engineering / pre-sales (depending on classification)
- Project Management without significant technical depth

For these roles, the General Employment Permit (GEP) is the relevant pathway,
with a Labour Market Needs Test required. The 2026 GEP thresholds are:

- Standard: **€36,605**
- Recent Irish-institution graduate: **€34,009**

## How the assistant should use this list

When the user asks "is X role on the Critical Skills List?":

1. The assistant should NOT make a definitive determination — CSOL classification
   uses official SOC 2010 occupation codes that the employer's HR/legal team
   determines on application
2. The assistant SHOULD identify whether the role broadly resembles a CSOL
   category, based on this knowledge base
3. The assistant SHOULD direct the user to the official current CSOL for
   verification
4. The assistant SHOULD note that ICT roles (software engineering, data, ML,
   cybersecurity) have been consistently on the CSOL through 2026 and are the
   most stable category for international CS graduates

## Common misconceptions to correct

### Misconception 1: "If I'm hired by Big Tech, I'm automatically on the CSOL"

Not necessarily. The CSOL applies to occupations, not employers. If a big tech
employer hires you for a non-CSOL role (e.g. a customer-success role with
technical elements), you would still go through the General Employment Permit
process. That said, **for SWE / Data / ML roles at any employer, CSOL eligibility
is highly likely.**

### Misconception 2: "Being on the CSOL guarantees my permit will be approved"

CSOL determines which permit type applies and waives certain procedural
requirements. It does not guarantee approval. The employer must still submit a
valid application with all required documentation, the salary must meet the
applicable threshold, and the candidate must meet experience/qualification
requirements.

### Misconception 3: "I need exactly the title 'Software Engineer' on my offer"

The CSOL is organised by SOC 2010 occupation codes, not exact job titles. A
"Backend Developer" role, "Junior Software Engineer" role, or "Platform
Engineer" role all map to relevant SWE codes on the CSOL. Employers determine
the correct SOC code on the permit application.

### Misconception 4: "The CSOL list never changes"

The list is reviewed periodically by DETE based on Expert Group on Future Skills
Needs analyses. Recent reviews (most recent published December 2023, with
periodic updates throughout 2024-2026) have added some occupations and refined
others. Always check the current version. ICT-related occupations have been
remarkably stable throughout this period.

### Misconception 5: "The salary threshold is fixed"

The thresholds **changed on 1 March 2026** under the new Roadmap, and further
adjustments are planned through 2030 with annual indexation to Central
Statistics Office (CSO) wage data. CSEP threshold rose from €38,000 to €40,904
in March 2026 (a 7.66% increase). Future increases are expected.

## What this list does NOT tell you

- Whether a specific employer will sponsor a permit (employer policy)
- The exact post-2026 threshold values for a given year (check official sources
  at the time of decision)
- Whether your specific qualifications match the occupation description (the
  employer's HR/immigration team determines this)
- How long a permit application will take in your specific case (varies)

For all of these, primary sources and qualified advice are necessary.
