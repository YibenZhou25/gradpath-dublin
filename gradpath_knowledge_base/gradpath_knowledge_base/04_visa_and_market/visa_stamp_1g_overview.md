# Stamp 1G · The Third-Level Graduate Programme

> Educational summary of Stamp 1G immigration permission in Ireland, written for
> international Master's-level CS students preparing to use this status as a
> bridge to longer-term work permission.
>
> **Last verified: April 2026.** Current as of the post-1-March-2026 employment
> permit threshold update. This is NOT legal advice and does NOT substitute for
> ISD or DETE guidance.

## What Stamp 1G is

Stamp 1G is a temporary immigration permission issued in Ireland to non-EU/EEA
graduates of Irish higher education institutions. It allows the holder to remain
in Ireland after their student visa ends, and to seek and undertake employment
**without an employment permit during the Stamp 1G period**.

The official name is the **Third-Level Graduate Programme**. The phrase "Stamp 1G"
refers to the immigration stamp affixed in the holder's passport during this
period.

## Who is eligible

Eligibility is set by the Department of Justice via Immigration Service Delivery
(ISD). General criteria:

1. The applicant must hold a current Stamp 2 student permission and an up-to-date
   immigration registration card (IRP)
2. The applicant must have successfully completed an eligible Irish award:
   - **Level 8 (Honours Bachelor's)**: 12 months of Stamp 1G (non-renewable)
   - **Level 9 (Master's) or Level 10 (PhD)**: 24 months total — granted as
     12 months initially, then renewed for a further 12 months
3. The applicant must apply within **6 months** of being notified by the awarding
   institution of successful completion
4. The applicant must not have already exceeded the seven-year limit on Stamp 2
   permission (eight years if availing of the Third Level Graduate Programme at
   Level 9 or above)

Always verify current eligibility criteria at:
- https://www.irishimmigration.ie/my-situation-has-changed-since-i-arrived-in-ireland/third-level-graduate-programme/
- Official PDF: https://www.irishimmigration.ie/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/third-level-graduate-programme.pdf

## What Stamp 1G permits

During the Stamp 1G period, the holder may:

- Seek employment in any sector (no occupation restrictions)
- Work full-time **up to 40 hours per week** without an employment permit
- Take internships or short-term roles
- Travel into and out of Ireland (subject to the visa rules of the holder's nationality)

The holder may NOT:

- Operate a business or be self-employed
- Settle in Ireland indefinitely under this status alone
- Bring dependants to Ireland under Stamp 1G alone
- Continue beyond the maximum duration without transitioning to another permission

## What Stamp 1G is meant to bridge

Stamp 1G is explicitly designed as a **transitional permission**. The intended
pathway for a Master's-level CS graduate is:

```
Stamp 2 (student) → Stamp 1G (graduate, up to 24 months) →
  Employment Permit + Stamp 1 (work) → Stamp 4 (after 21+ months on CSEP)
```

Within the 24 months, the holder is expected to:

1. Find an employer willing to apply for an employment permit on their behalf
2. Have the employer apply for either:
   - **Critical Skills Employment Permit (CSEP)** — preferred route, no Labour
     Market Needs Test, immediate family reunification, fast Stamp 4 path
   - **General Employment Permit (GEP)** — fallback, requires Labour Market Needs
     Test, longer path to Stamp 4
3. Once the employment permit is granted, register with ISD for **Stamp 1**
   permission

After **21 months** on Stamp 1 with a Critical Skills Permit, the holder may
apply directly to the Department of Justice for **Stamp 4**, which permits
residence and work without further permit sponsorship.

> Important update for 2026: Critical Skills permit holders apply for Stamp 4
> directly to the Department of Justice without needing a renewal permit through
> DETE. This is a meaningful procedural simplification.

## Critical timing considerations

### When Stamp 1G ends

The Stamp 1G clock is fixed by the registration date. A holder who has not
secured an employment permit application in process by the end of their 1G
period faces a difficult position. The Stamp 1G **second-year extension** is
not automatic — the graduate must demonstrate to ISD that they have taken
appropriate steps to access suitable graduate-level employment (e.g. attendance
at job interviews, signing up with graduate employment agencies).

### How long employment permit applications take in 2026

Standard processing times for employment permits in 2026:

- **Critical Skills Employment Permit**: 4–8 weeks standard
- **CSEP via Trusted Partner employers** (large multinationals like Google, Meta,
  Stripe, Microsoft, Amazon): often **2–4 weeks** with priority routing
- **General Employment Permit**: 5–10 weeks (longer due to Labour Market Needs
  Test requirements)

**Practical implication**: a graduate aiming to be employed continuously on the
correct visa should typically begin job-hunting **6 months before** the Stamp 1G
end date, not in the final weeks. With a Trusted Partner employer the runway can
be shorter, but planning for delays remains wise.

### Working under Stamp 1G while a permit application is pending

Because Stamp 1G itself permits employment for up to 40 hours a week, a graduate
can start working under Stamp 1G while the employer's permit application is
being processed. This is the standard pattern at most major employers.

## Salary thresholds for the next step (employment permits, from 1 March 2026)

When transitioning from Stamp 1G to an employment permit, the offered salary
must meet the relevant Minimum Annual Remuneration (MAR) threshold.

**Current thresholds (effective 1 March 2026):**

| Permit type | Standard threshold | Recent-graduate threshold (within 12 months of graduation) |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| Critical Skills Employment Permit (with relevant degree) | **€40,904** | **€36,848** |
| Critical Skills Employment Permit (without degree, with experience) | €68,911 | n/a |
| General Employment Permit | **€36,605** | **€34,009** (Irish institution graduates) |
| Intra-Company Transfer Employment Permit | €49,523 | n/a |

The **recent-graduate threshold** (re-introduced from 1 March 2026) is a
critical adjustment: a Stamp 1G holder transitioning into their first permanent
role within 12 months of graduation can qualify with a salary about €4,000 lower
than the standard threshold. This is a meaningful cost-of-employment reduction
for employers and improves graduate hiring economics.

### Important caveat for the second year of Stamp 1G

If a graduate has been granted a second year of Stamp 1G permission and their
graduation date has gone beyond 12 months, they may **not** be eligible for the
lower graduate thresholds. They will default back to the higher standard
thresholds. **Timing is critical**: graduates planning to transition to an
employment permit should aim to do so before the 12-month-post-graduation
window closes, where possible.

## Common scenarios for CS Master's graduates in 2026

### Scenario A — Offer from a Trusted Partner multinational

Examples: Google, Meta, Stripe, Microsoft, Amazon, Apple, Datadog, HubSpot,
Salesforce, LinkedIn. These employers have streamlined permit operations and
priority processing. Standard CSEP application, typically processed in
2–4 weeks. Salary almost always exceeds the €40,904 threshold by a wide margin.

### Scenario B — Offer from a smaller Dublin tech employer with sponsorship history

The employer has experience but smaller volume. CSEP application typical
4–8 week processing. Salary check: confirm the offer meets €40,904 (or €36,848
if you graduated within 12 months).

### Scenario C — Offer from an employer where the role is NOT on the Critical Skills List

The employer must complete a Labour Market Needs Test (28-day advertising
period) and apply for a General Employment Permit. The relevant threshold is
€36,605 (or €34,009 for recent Irish graduates). Total timeline including LMNT
is typically 5–10 weeks plus the advertising period.

### Scenario D — No offer secured in time

The graduate's options narrow:
- Apply for the second year of Stamp 1G if Level 9 or above and not yet used
- Switch to another study programme (back to Stamp 2)
- Return to home country and apply for an employment permit from abroad if an
  offer materialises later

This scenario is preventable with early planning.

## What the assistant should always remind the user

1. Salary threshold values changed on 1 March 2026 and will continue to change
   through 2030 under the published Roadmap. Always verify current values at
   https://enterprise.gov.ie/.
2. The Critical Skills Occupations List is reviewed periodically and roles can
   be added or removed between reviews.
3. Employer sponsorship willingness varies and is not always disclosed up-front.
   Ask explicitly during the interview process before accepting an offer.
4. The 6-month application window after award notification is a hard deadline.
5. The 12-month-post-graduation window matters for unlocking the lower
   recent-graduate salary threshold.
6. This document is educational. For specific legal advice, consult a qualified
   immigration solicitor.
